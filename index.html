What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure of body fat based on your weight and height. It is a widely used diagnostic tool that can help determine if a person is underweight, normal weight, overweight or obese.

Why do medical professionals use BMI?

Medical professionals use BMI as a quick and easy way to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems. It is not a perfect measure of body fat, but it is an inexpensive and noninvasive alternative to more accurate methods of measuring body fat such as skinfold thickness measurements or bioelectrical impedance.

How to interpret BMI results?

The World Health Organization (WHO) has established the following BMI ranges for adults:

  • Underweight: less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: 18.5-24.9
  • Overweight: 25-29.9
  • Obese: 30 or greater

If your BMI falls outside of the normal range, you should consult a medical professional for advice on how to manage your weight and improve your health.
